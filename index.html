I studied at a traditional Chinese academy as a child, without attending a regular primary school. My daily lessons included reciting the Three Character Classic and the Thousand Character Essay, and copying oracle bone and bronze inscriptions with a brush.

Later, when I encountered computers and programming, I was amazed to find a strange similarity between the compact structure of classical Chinese and programming languages. This discovery inspired me to use technology to interpret classical texts, especially ancient mathematical works.

  • Convert classical math problems from The Nine Chapters into modern expressions
  • Visualize the relationship between character evolution and math symbols
  • Create interactive animations to solve problems found in ancient texts

These tools aim not to replace traditional learning but to build a bridge that makes these cultural treasures more accessible to everyone.
